GREEK FREELANCER TAX CALCULATOR
SAMPLE CALCULATIONS AND WORKED EXAMPLES
================================================================================

This document provides detailed, step-by-step calculations for various income
scenarios to help users understand how Greek freelancer taxes are computed.
All calculations follow Greek tax law as of 2024.

================================================================================
TAX COMPONENTS OVERVIEW
================================================================================

Greek freelancers are subject to three main obligations:

1. INCOME TAX (Progressive)
   - Applied to taxable income (gross income - deductible expenses)
   - Uses progressive brackets: 9%, 22%, 28%, 36%, 44%
   - Only the income within each bracket is taxed at that rate

2. VAT (Value Added Tax)
   - Fixed rate: 24%
   - Applied to gross income
   - Collected from clients and remitted to tax authorities
   - NOT part of personal tax burden (pass-through tax)

3. EFKA SOCIAL SECURITY
   - Total rate: 20%
   - Applied to gross income (before expense deductions)
   - Breakdown:
     * Main Insurance: 13.33%
     * Additional Contributions: 6.67%
   - Provides healthcare, pension, and social benefits

   - EFKA is €2,400 on gross income

   - Actual take-home is effectively €330 (€12,000 - €9,000 - €2,670)
   
   This scenario illustrates why tracking expenses and managing
   cash flow is critical for freelancers with high operating costs.

KEY TAKEAWAYS
================================================================================

1. PROGRESSIVE TAXATION WORKS IN YOUR FAVOR:
   - Higher income doesn't mean all income is taxed at the highest rate
   - Only the portion in each bracket is taxed at that bracket's rate
   - Effective tax rates are typically much lower than marginal rates

2. DEDUCTIBLE EXPENSES PROVIDE SIGNIFICANT TAX SAVINGS:
   - Example 2: €5,000 expenses saved €2,300 in taxes (46% return)
   - Example 3: €10,000 expenses saved €3,800 in taxes (38% return)
   - The higher your tax bracket, the more valuable expense deductions become

3. EFKA IS A FIXED BURDEN ON GROSS INCOME:
   - Always 20% of gross income, regardless of expenses
   - Cannot be reduced through deductions
   - Can be disproportionately high for low-margin businesses (see Example 4)
   - Plan for this in pricing and cash flow management

4. VAT IS A PASS-THROUGH TAX:
   - Not part of your personal tax burden
   - Collected from clients and remitted to authorities
   - Include VAT in your invoices (add 24% to your fee)
   - Keep VAT funds separate for remittance

5. PAYMENT FLEXIBILITY:
   - Choose monthly, quarterly, or annual payments
   - Monthly payments help with cash flow management
   - Quarterly is common for many freelancers
   - Annual requires good financial discipline

6. ACCURATE RECORD-KEEPING IS ESSENTIAL:
   - Track all business expenses throughout the year
   - Keep receipts and invoices for at least 5 years
   - Proper documentation maximizes legitimate deductions
   - Consider accounting software or professional help

VERIFICATION INSTRUCTIONS
================================================================================

All calculations in this document can be manually verified using:

1. Income Tax Brackets (2024):
   €0-€10,000: 9% | €10,001-€20,000: 22% | €20,001-€30,000: 28%
   €30,001-€40,000: 36% | €40,001+: 44%

2. VAT Rate: 24%

3. EFKA Social Security: 20% (13.33% + 6.67%)

To verify any calculation:
- Start with gross income
- Subtract deductible expenses to get taxable income
- Apply progressive tax brackets to taxable income
- Calculate EFKA on gross income (not taxable income)
- Calculate VAT on gross income
- Sum income tax + EFKA for total taxes
- Subtract total taxes from gross income for net income
